# Digital Product: Idea → Launch (7 steps)

## Core philosophy

Do not spend 30 days building before knowing anyone wants it.
**Find it → validate it → build the smallest useful version → launch fast →
listen → make v2 better.** Every step should be concrete enough to act on today.

Default end goal: a low-priced front-end product that creates a fast, real result
AND funnels buyers into a recurring offer (e.g. a monthly membership) when one exists.

## Inputs to collect first

Ask only for what's missing, in one message:

1. **Background / niche** — skills, experience, audience, and what people already ask them about. The more specific, the less generic the output.
2. **Audience** — who exactly buys (demographics + the felt problem).
3. **Existing offer** — any membership/course/coaching to funnel into, and its price.
4. **Main platform** — Instagram / TikTok / Facebook / X / LinkedIn / email (for Step 7).
5. **Output location** — default `content/digital-product/` in this repo, one file per step.

If the user says "use what's in the repo," read `README.md`, `content/formula.md`,
`content/hooks.md`, and `content/style_guide.md` to infer niche, audience, and voice.

## Output

Write one Markdown file per step into `content/digital-product/` (plus a `README.md`
index), then deliver a tight chat summary. Match the brand's voice. Keep every
deliverable specific — no filler, no empty hype, no fake urgency.

## The 7 steps

### 1 — Find the idea (`01-product-ideas.md`)
Act as a digital product strategist. Generate **10** ideas the user could build in
under 7 days. For each: target buyer · painful problem · product format · why they
pay · fastest version to launch · easiest marketing · price range · difficulty 1–10.
Avoid generic ideas; every idea solves a clear problem. **Rank three ways** — by
speed, by demand, by simplicity — then name one overall winner to carry forward.

### 2 — Validate demand (`02-validation.md`)
Act as a brutally honest validator on the winning idea. Break down: buyer pain
(severity, frequency, current alternatives) · demand (what they search, existing
products, what they already pay) · competition (done well / done badly / open
angle) · product strength (why buy / why ignore / what makes it more valuable) ·
verdict (rate 1–10, build or not, strongest version of the offer).
**Use web search and separate VERIFIED facts from ASSUMPTIONS.** Do not compliment
weak ideas. List sources as markdown links.

### 3 — Design the MVP (`03-mvp-design.md`)
Act as a product designer. Define the smallest useful version: the exact result
the buyer gets · format · sections/modules · what must be included · what to
remove · the order to follow · a completion checklist · final deliverables · the
fastest version buildable today · the better version for later. One clear outcome,
no extras.

### 4 — Build the product (`04-the-product.md`)
Act as the product builder. Write the **full first version**. For every section:
clear title · simple explanation · practical examples · steps to follow ·
templates/scripts/prompts/checklists where useful · no filler · scannable. End
with: a quick-start page, table of contents, completion checklist, and a "what to
do next" section that bridges to the recurring offer. It should be usable
immediately. Include a safety/disclaimer note for sensitive niches (relationships,
health, finance, legal).

### 5 — Package it (`05-packaging.md`)
Act as a packaging expert. Deliver: 10 product names · strongest subtitle · main
promise · target buyer in one sentence · 5 biggest benefits · cover concept ·
visual style · color direction · tone of voice · final product description. Then a
**cover brief** ready for Canva / an image AI / a designer (dimensions, image
prompt, layout, type, palette, what to avoid). Clean, specific, premium — no vague
names or hype.

### 6 — Write the sales page (`06-sales-page.md`)
Act as a direct-response copywriter. Produce: headline · subheadline · problem
section · why current solutions fail · what the product helps them do · what's
included · who it's for · who it's NOT for · benefits · objections + answers · CTA
· short FAQ. Rules: clear, not scammy, no fake urgency, no unsupported claims,
value obvious in under 30 seconds.

### 7 — Launch & improve (`07-launch-plan.md`)
Act as a launch strategist. Build a **7-day plan** for the user's main platform —
each day: content angle · hook · post idea · CTA · best format · objection to
handle · proof to show. Plus: 10 launch hooks · 5 short posts · 3 educational
posts · 3 story posts · 3 urgency posts (no fake scarcity) · 5 DM replies for
interested buyers · 1 follow-up for non-buyers. Keep it executable by one person.
Close with a short "version 2" note: read DMs and refund reasons, turn the most
common question into the next product or sales-page section, then relaunch.

## Guardrails

- **Launch fast, then iterate.** Steps 1→7 are a loop, not a one-shot. Push toward
  the smallest shippable version; resist scope creep.
- **No fake scarcity, no unsupported claims, no hype.** Urgency comes from the cost
  of inaction, not fake timers.
- **Honesty in validation.** If the idea is weak, say so and propose a stronger one.
- **Sensitive niches** (relationships, health, money, legal): include a disclaimer
  and never imply professional/clinical treatment.
- **Match the user's voice**, not a generic info-product voice.
