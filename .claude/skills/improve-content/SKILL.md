# Improve Content Skill

## What this skill does

Takes any draft content (reel script, hook, caption, blog, carousel copy) and runs it through the Tarisha Starr content quality system. Outputs a scored audit, specific fixes, and a rewritten version.

## Before improving, load these reference files

Read these files to internalise the voice, strategy, and standards:

1. `content/style_guide.md` — Voice rules. No em-dashes, always contractions, NZ spelling, conversational tone.
2. `content/formula.md` — The pain-hook + emotional-truth formula.
3. `content/hooks.md` — The hook bank for pairing and inspiration.
4. `content/curiosity-loops-guide.md` — The 5 curiosity tools (open loops, delayed payoff, micro questions, pattern interrupts, momentum transitions), the 3-touch rule, and the non-clickbait test.
5. `content/reel-differentiation-viral-guide.md` — 2026 algorithm data, the Tarisha reel template, production upgrades, and the DM-send formula.
6. `content/retention-prompts.md` — 10 audit prompts: pacing audit (1), payoff audit (2), format optimisation (3), MrBeast retention review (4), curiosity loop check (5), voice consistency (6), improve pacing (7), strengthen payoff with 5 ending types (8), one idea into 5 formats (9), final MrBeast-style rewrite (10).
7. `content/hooks-10-angles.md` — 200 hook variations across 10 angles for A/B testing inspiration.

## The improvement process

### Step 1: Score the draft (1-10 on each)

| Criteria | What to check |
|---|---|
| Hook strength | Does it stop the scroll in under 1.5 seconds? Does it open a gap? |
| First 3 seconds | Is text on screen before voice? Is there a 3-second hold mechanism? |
| Curiosity gap | Does the audience need to keep watching to resolve something? |
| Pacing | Does every sentence earn its place? Any drag, stall, or filler? |
| Repetition | Any idea said twice without adding new value? |
| Emotional pull | Does she FEEL something, or just learn something? |
| Payoff | Does the resolution match the promise of the hook? |
| Ending | Does it drive a share, save, comment, or caption read? |

### Step 2: Identify weak spots

For each weak moment:
- **Why it loses attention** (be specific: "this line restates what was already said")
- **What to cut** (the exact words)
- **What to replace it with** (the exact replacement)
- **How to make the next line pull forward** (specific curiosity device)

### Step 3: Rewrite with these rules

**Voice rules (from style_guide.md):**
- No em-dashes (—) or en-dashes (–). Use commas, periods, ellipses.
- Always contractions (don't, can't, isn't, won't, you've, she's, it's).
- British/NZ spelling (realise, colour, favourite, recognise, apologise, optimise).
- Sound like spoken word, not written prose. Fragments OK. Run-ons OK.
- Personify the body as "she" — "she's been calling you home."
- Pet names welcome: "darling," "my love."
- No therapist-speak, coaching cliches, or performative empathy.

**Curiosity rules (from curiosity-loops-guide.md):**
- Exactly 3 curiosity touches per reel (hook, mid-point reset, close/loop).
- Open loops must pay off (in reel, caption, or next post).
- Micro questions create internal engagement (1-2 per reel max).
- Pattern interrupts reset attention (1 per reel max, tone/pace shift preferred).
- Momentum transitions pull forward ("But here's what nobody tells you...").

**Retention rules (from reel-differentiation-viral-guide.md):**
- Text on screen in the first frame, before voice begins.
- 3-second hold rate is the #1 predictor of reach.
- 10-second reel at 80% retention > 60-second reel at 30%.
- DM sends are the #1 algorithm signal. Design for "send to one specific person."
- Caption adds a new layer, never repeats the reel.
- End with a question (2-3x more comments than a command).

**The Tarisha difference (from reel-differentiation-viral-guide.md):**
- Create recognition, not education. She should feel CAUGHT, not taught.
- Bridge science AND body wisdom in one piece.
- Centre the 40+ woman exclusively.
- Sound like a friend at 11pm, not a therapist at 2pm.
- Brand: "Seen. Not fixed."

### Step 4: Hook alternatives

After rewriting, provide 3 alternative hooks using different angles from hooks-10-angles.md:
- One shock angle
- One curiosity angle
- One pain or contrarian angle

## What NOT to do

- Don't add em-dashes. Ever.
- Don't make it longer. Make it tighter.
- Don't add features or abstractions the draft doesn't need.
- Don't use "Furthermore," "However," "Moreover," or any stiff transitions.
- Don't add "Research shows" or "Studies suggest" unless the user's draft is explicitly science-based.
- Don't over-loop. Three curiosity touches. Not five.
- Don't make it robotic or formulaic. It should feel like a real person talking.
