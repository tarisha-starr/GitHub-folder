# From Roommates to Lovers Membership: Acquisition Engine

A predictable lead system that feeds the $97/month membership.

---

## Context

- **Niche:** women 40+ in long-term relationships. Long polite marriages, more love than spark. NZ first, AU second, US/UK third.
- **Primary platform:** Instagram (Reels for reach, DMs for nurture). YouTube as the trust and search layer. Podcast as the long-form depth play. Email as the close.
- **Offer:** $97/month membership (entry $27 Lovers Deck, core $97 membership, premium $2,400 quarterly intensive).
- **Current traffic:** medium. Existing audience on Instagram, podcast, and email. Quiz funnel built (theloveadventure.com/quiz). Buyers list still small.

## 1. Highest leverage traffic source for this niche

**Instagram Reels is the lead source.** Women 40+ live on Instagram. Reels are still the cheapest reach in the platform. Tarisha to camera, intimate spoken-word, lands hard with this audience.

**YouTube long-form is the trust source.** Same women search "we feel like roommates" at 11pm and find no one talking the way Tarisha talks. Owning that search query is a long compounding asset.

**Podcast is the depth source.** Existing podcast keeps the relationship warm with the people who are not on social all day.

**Email is the close.** Buyers list gets the offer, the urgency, the live teach reminders.

**Mark's network is the unfair advantage.** He has reach into the men's work and couples retreat side. Cross-posts move buyers into the Love Adventure pipeline both directions.

**One sentence:** Reels for reach, DMs and email for nurture, quiz for diagnosis, $27 Deck for first buy, $97 membership for retention.

## 3. Five content angles that attract BUYERS, not just views

Hormozi rule: write to the wallet, not the algorithm. Each angle below names the exact buyer state and offers her a next step.

**Angle 1: The Two Patterns.**
"You're not fighting. You're also not having sex. Those are the same problem in two different rooms." Diagnostic content. Pulls the buyer who recognises herself. Highest intent.

**Angle 2: The Polite Years.**
"You'd never call it a crisis. That's exactly why no one's helping you." Names the loneliness she has not let herself name. Pulls the buyer who has been hiding inside a good marriage.

**Angle 3: Start Solo.**
"Your partner does not have to be ready for you to begin." Removes the single biggest objection in the niche. Pulls the buyer who has been waiting on him.

**Angle 4: Fifteen Minutes.**
"Show me fifteen minutes a day for one week a month. I'll show you a marriage with sex in it." Speaks to the no-time woman. Pulls the buyer who is overwhelmed.

**Angle 5: It's Not Too Late.**
Tarisha's own evergreen voice. "A vibrant life full of intimacy after 40, 50, 60 is your divine right." Pulls the buyer who has decided, somewhere inside, that part of her life is over. (It isn't.)

**Posting mix for buyer-intent (not vanity reach):** 3 of 5 Reels per week are Angle 1 or 2 (diagnostic). 1 is Angle 3 or 4 (objection-killer). 1 is Angle 5 (emotional rallying cry).

## 4. Outbound, partnerships, collaborations

**DM nurture (warm).**
Every Reel CTA is a comment prompt per Tarisha's rule. Example: "Comment 'roommates' and I'll send you the quiz." The DM that goes out is the quiz link plus one sentence. After the quiz, the funnel takes over.

**Podcast guesting (warm to cold).**
Target list: 30 podcasts a quarter, in three buckets.
- Midlife women (e.g. perimenopause, second-half, empty-nest pods)
- Relationship and intimacy (couples therapy, sex-positive)
- Mark's adjacent world (men's work, couples retreats) for the partner-half story

One pitch a week, ten weeks. Track in a sheet.

**Partnerships (high leverage, low volume).**
- One IG live a month with a complementary practitioner (yoga teacher, breathwork, perimenopause specialist) in AU/NZ.
- Cross-newsletter swaps with two aligned women's-work accounts per quarter.
- Mark's network: bring two men's-work figures into a Love Adventure conversation per quarter, post the recording.

**No outbound DMs to cold strangers.** Hormozi-style cold outreach reads off-brand for Tarisha and burns trust. She does inbound and partnerships only.

## 5. Lead capture system

**The funnel, top to bottom:**

```
Reel (comment prompt)
  → DM auto-reply with quiz link
  → Quiz (Roommates to Lovers, 6 questions, archetype result)
  → Archetype result page (e.g. The Roommates) with built-in 60-min $97 offer
  → If no purchase: 7-day email nurture (existing sequence in Notion)
  → If purchase of $27 Deck OR $97 membership: buyer list, next-step ladder
  → Membership delivery: monthly live, fresh practice, members' space
```

**The three capture points and what each one does:**

| Capture point | Job | Conversion target |
|---|---|---|
| Comment prompt on Reels | Get the DM started, no friction | 1 to 3% of viewers |
| Quiz start | Identify the archetype, get the email | 60% of quiz starters complete |
| Quiz-result page offer | First purchase: $27 Deck or $97 membership | 2 to 5% of completers |

**Landing pages:**

- `/quiz`: the diagnostic entry. Single CTA: take the quiz.
- `/quiz/roommates`: the archetype result, with the $97 membership offer baked in and a 60-min timer. (Already drafted in Notion as the $97 sales page, repurposed for membership.)
- `/from-roommates-to-lovers`: the standalone direct-traffic page (for podcast guesting, partnerships, email).
- `/members`: login + dashboard for paying members.

**Email infrastructure:** HighLevel, as already chosen. Two lists: subscribers (freebie + quiz) and buyers ($27 or $97). The buyer list is the asset.

## 6. Optimised for the three rules

**Consistency:** Monday-batch the week's Reels. Tue/Thu newsletter slots, non-negotiable. One podcast a week. The system survives bad weeks because the input is small.

**Simplicity:** one funnel, one quiz, three landing pages, two email lists. Nothing more.

**Scalability:** Reels compound. The quiz compounds (no extra cost per new lead). The membership compounds (each retained member is monthly recurring). Partnerships are linear but high-leverage. Tarisha's time-per-dollar drops with every new member.
