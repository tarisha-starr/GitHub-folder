# The Formula

**Pain hook + emotional truth**

The hook names the pain she's been carrying quietly. The emotional truth
reframes it so she feels seen, not diagnosed.

## Examples

- "You're not broken. You're exhausted."
- "You don't hate sex. You hate pressure."
- "You can love him and still feel lonely."
- "Your body didn't betray you. It adapted."
- "The spark didn't die. It got buried under years of being useful."

That last one has teeth. Use it.

## How to write your own

1. **Name the surface story** she tells herself ("I'm broken", "I hate sex",
   "I don't love him anymore").
2. **Replace it with the deeper truth** ("you're exhausted", "you hate
   pressure", "the closeness got buried").
3. **Keep both halves short.** Two sentences. No qualifiers. No softening.

## What to avoid

- Advice voice ("you should…", "try…")
- Clinical voice ("studies show…", "research suggests…")
- Performative empathy ("I see you, queen")

The hook should sound like something she'd whisper to herself at 11pm — not
something a coach would post.
