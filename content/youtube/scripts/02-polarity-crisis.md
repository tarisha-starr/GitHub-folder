## 3. The script (with ElevenLabs VO direction markers)

Markers: `[pause]` `[slower]` `[whisper]` `[breath]` `[softer]`

### HOOK (0:00 to 0:05)
**Visual:** Couch. Emma and Mark in identical grey marl joggers and white t-shirts. Sharing one phone. Not touching.

**VO [whisper, slower]:**
> When did your husband become... your flatmate? [pause]

### SETUP (0:05 to 0:30)
**Visual:** Title card. Cuts to whiteboard meeting.

**VO:**
> Her name's Emma. [pause] She's been married to Mark for 17 years. Two kids. A renovated villa. They split everything 50/50. They share a Google calendar that colour-codes the dog walks. [softer] They had sex 14 months ago. Or maybe 15. Neither one remembers.

### ACT 1 — The Partnership Trap (0:30 to 2:00)
**VO:**
> Here's what nobody told Emma about modern marriage. [pause]
>
> They high-fived after the meal-plan meeting. Actually high-fived. [breath] He called her 'partner.' Like business. Like equity. Like... not lover.
>
> "Night, mate," he said. [pause] "Night," she said back. [slower] Mate. He called her mate. And neither of them noticed.
>
> [softer, slower] They'd built a partnership so equal, so well-managed, so identical... that the polarity that pulled them together in their twenties? Had nowhere left to live.

### ACT 2 — The Deida Reveal (2:00 to 4:00)
**Visual:** Emma at sink, AirPods in, hands frozen in soap.

**Podcast VO (different voice, slightly distant):**
> David Deida says the ground rule of attraction is polarity. The gap between masculine and feminine. The charge between two opposites. When you collapse that gap in the name of equality, you can build a magnificent partnership. But you can't build desire.

**VO [Tarisha, warm]:**
> Polarity, darling. [pause] It's the thing nobody warned you about. The masculine and the feminine aren't roles. They're energies. And they're meant to be different.
>
> Same pole? Push apart. [pause] Opposite pole? Snap together. [slower] It's not gender. It's not 1950s. It's biology. And it's what Deida calls the polarity principle.
>
> [softer] Emma and Mark didn't kill their sex life by fighting. They killed it by becoming identical. Two co-managers of a small business they call a marriage. [pause] And when you both wear the masculine... nobody's home for the feminine to be received by.

**Emma [whispered, kitchen floor]:** Oh god. We're flatmates.

### ACT 3 — The Contrast Couple (4:00 to 6:00)
**VO:**
> Two days later, Emma had dinner with her friend Lisa.

**Visual:** Lisa cooking in cream linen dress. Tom enters, claims her with a real kiss.

**Emma [whispered]:** He still does that?
**Lisa [smiling]:** Every single night.

**VO [softer]:**
> Lisa hadn't lost herself. Tom hadn't lost his edge. They didn't share an operating system. They had something else. [pause] Polarity. The thing Emma had been negotiating away for 17 years and calling it equality.
>
> Emma realised something on the drive home. She hadn't worn a dress in years. She hadn't been touched first in years. [slower] She'd been so committed to being a 'good partner'... she'd forgotten how to be a woman.

### ACT 4 — The Reclamation (6:00 to 7:30)
**VO:**
> She didn't tell Mark. She didn't book a couples therapist. Not yet. She did something smaller. And bigger. And harder.
>
> She stopped carrying the masculine in her own home. She put it down. [softer] And her body, darling? The one she'd been overriding for 17 years to be a 'good partner'? Her body started to come back.

**Mark [quietly, seeing her in the dress]:** Em.
**Emma:** Hi.
**Mark:** ...You look beautiful.
**Emma:** I know.

**VO:**
> She didn't seduce him. [pause] She seduced herself first. He just... remembered how to follow.
>
> [slower] That's the secret Deida tries to teach the world. The feminine doesn't have to do the masculine's job. She just has to come back online. And when she does? The masculine in him remembers what it's for.
>
> [softer] He claimed her at the table that night. Then in the kitchen. Then at the bottom of the stairs... before they ever made it to the bed.

### ENDING (7:30 to 8:00)
**Visual:** Morning. Emma in his t-shirt. Mark wraps around her from behind.

**VO [direct, intimate]:**
> If you're watching this and you just realised you've been married to your business partner for 8 years? Or 18? Or 28? [pause] That's her, darling. Your feminine. She's not gone. She's underneath the spreadsheet. Underneath the joggers. Underneath the 'mate' and the 'partner' and the 'we got this.' [slower] She's waiting.
>
> [warm] Comment 'polarity' if this is you. I'll send you the next part of the love adventure. Because Emma's story... isn't where it ends. It's where it starts to feel like a marriage again.

**End card teaser:** "The 3 Things Every Husband Needs From You" and "Why He Pulls Away the Closer You Get."

## 8. CapCut edit notes

- **Pacing:** 3 to 5 seconds per scene clip. Don't rush. Hold emotional beats.
- **Transitions:** Soft cross-dissolve between most scenes. Hard cut on the magnet snap (scene 9) and the kiss (scene 19). No whip pans.
- **Music bed:** Epidemic Sound "Tender Hours" or "Quiet Comfort" playlists. Piano + cello. Drop music ENTIRELY during the kitchen-floor whisper (scene 11) so the silence hits. Swell back in for the bath (scene 17).
- **Sound design:** Kitchen sounds (slicing, kettle, dish water), candle flicker (scene 17), soft door close at the end of the bedroom implication, footsteps when Mark enters (scene 18).
- **Subtitles:** Always on. Warm cream serif font, drop-shadow, dusty rose highlight on emphasis words (LONELY, POLARITY, FEMININE, MATE).
- **VO ducking:** Music drops to -18db under VO, comes back to -10db in transitions.
- **Total target runtime:** 8:00 +/- 10 seconds.
