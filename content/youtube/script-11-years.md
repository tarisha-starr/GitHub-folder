# Full Script: "She Hadn't Felt Desire in 11 Years... Then One Tuesday Changed Everything"

Channel: The Love Adventure. Format: faceless animated story, roughly 7 to 9 minutes.
Companion to `youtube-monetization-roadmap.md`, Section 4 (the high-retention script system).

Voice rules: UK/NZ spelling, no dashes, no "real" in copy, no clickbait. The body is "she".
Pet names "darling" and "my love". Warm, direct, coach-to-friend. No AI clone of Tarisha:
this is voiceover over animation, so her face never appears.

Markings: **[PAUSE]** hold the beat. **[EMPHASIS]** land the word. **[ENERGY UP]** and
**[ENERGY DOWN]** shift the pace. **[SCENE]** the visual change (the pattern interrupt).

The retention spine this follows:
- Hook in the first 3 seconds, no intro.
- A clear promise by 0:15.
- A pattern interrupt roughly every 20 to 30 seconds (marked **[SCENE]**).
- A genuine tension beat in the middle third, not only at the open.
- Ends on a reason to watch the next video, plus a comment-keyword CTA.

Read it aloud once before recording the voiceover. Any line that is dull to say is dull to
watch. Cut it.

## COLD OPEN (0:00 to 0:20)

**[SCENE: a woman's hands, still, resting in a sink of cooling dishwater. Steam thinning.
Late afternoon light through a kitchen window. No face.]**

**[ENERGY DOWN, almost quiet]**

Eleven years.

**[PAUSE]**

That is how long it had been since Claire felt anything move in her body when he touched
her. Eleven years of a hand on her shoulder that landed like a note left on the fridge.
Kind. Familiar. **[EMPHASIS]** Nothing.

**[PAUSE]**

She thought she was broken. Darling, she was not broken.

---

## THE PROMISE (0:20 to 0:45)

**[SCENE: slow pull back. Claire at the sink, shoulders rounded, the kitchen tidy and quiet
around her. A child's drawing on the fridge, curling at one corner.]**

**[ENERGY UP, warmer]**

By the end of this, Claire feels one small thing again. On an ordinary Tuesday. At the
kitchen sink, with her hands in the water. And I want you to notice the exact moment it
turns, because it is not the moment you would expect.

**[PAUSE]**

It is not a date night. It is not lingerie. It is not him doing anything different at all.
It is one question she asks her own body, for the first time in a decade. Stay with her.
The turn is quiet, and it is the whole thing.

---

## ACT ONE: HOW SHE WENT QUIET (0:45 to 2:30)

**[SCENE: quick warm montage, younger Claire. Dancing in a kitchen. Laughing with her head
back. A hand pulling her toward a bedroom door. Colour saturated, alive.]**

**[ENERGY UP]**

She was not always quiet. There was a version of Claire who wanted things. Who reached
first. Who did not have to be coaxed into her own life.

**[SCENE: the colour drains, slow. The same kitchen, years on. A pram. Then a school bag.
Then a teenager's trainers by the door. The light goes flat.]**

**[ENERGY DOWN]**

And then life happened, the way it happens to good women. Babies. A mortgage. His long
hours, then her long hours. A mother who got ill. A teenager who stopped talking. Somewhere
in all of it, Claire made a quiet decision she never noticed making.

**[PAUSE]**

Someone had to hold it. So she folded herself up small, and she held it.

**[SCENE: close on her hands, folding a jumper, smoothing it flat. Over and over.]**

Her body noticed the folding before she did. **[EMPHASIS]** Bodies always do. When a woman
spends eleven years being needed and never wanted, her body learns the lesson. Desire is
not safe here. Desire is one more thing being asked of her. So she goes quiet. Not out of
spite, my love. Out of self-protection.

**[PAUSE]**

Claire called it "I'm just tired." Her body called it something truer. She had simply stopped
being asked what she wanted. So she stopped answering.

---

## ACT TWO: THE PATTERN, AND THE PRESSURE (2:30 to 4:15)

**[SCENE: bedroom, dim. A bedside lamp. His hand reaching across the duvet at 10:47 on the
clock. Her body still, facing away, eyes open.]**

**[ENERGY DOWN, honest]**

Here is the part no one says out loud. It was not that Claire did not love him. She did.
It was that every reach for her had become a request. A small bill arriving at the end of a
day where she had already paid everyone else.

**[SCENE: hard cut. Daylight. A magazine cover, a friend's voice, captions flying past on a
phone screen. "Spice it up." "Plan a date night." "Prioritise intimacy."]**

**[ENERGY UP, a touch sharp, pattern interrupt]**

And everyone had advice. Spice it up. Book a hotel. Try harder. **[EMPHASIS]** Try harder.
As if the woman who had been trying hard for twenty years just needed to try a little harder
at the one thing that was quietly breaking her heart.

**[PAUSE]**

**[ENERGY DOWN]**

None of it worked. Of course it did not. You cannot pressure a body back into wanting. You
cannot schedule your way to desire. Every "try harder" told Claire the same thing her whole
life had been telling her: what you want does not matter, only that you deliver.

**[SCENE: Claire in the bathroom, the door locked, back against it, just breathing. The tap
running so no one hears.]**

So she started locking the bathroom door. Just to breathe. Just to have one square metre of
the house where no one needed anything from her. She did not have the words for it yet. But
that locked door was the first thing her body had asked for in years. **[PAUSE]** And she
gave it. Quietly. Guiltily. But she gave it.

---

## ACT THREE: THE TUESDAY (4:15 to 6:15). The tension beat.

**[SCENE: the kitchen sink again. Ordinary Tuesday. Hands in the water. The house empty for
once. A clock ticking.]**

**[ENERGY DOWN, slow, this is the middle-third tension, let it breathe]**

And then, one Tuesday, nothing happened. That is the honest truth of it. Nothing happened.

The kids were out. He was at work. The house was empty in a way it almost never was. Claire
stood at the sink with her hands in the warm water, and she noticed something that stopped
her cold.

**[PAUSE, hold]**

She could not remember the last time she wanted anything.

**[EMPHASIS]** Not him. **[PAUSE]** Anything.

**[SCENE: very still. The water. Her hands. Steam. Nothing moves.]**

Not a holiday. Not a meal. Not a morning. She had been so busy being the answer to everyone
else's wanting that her own had gone somewhere she could not find it. And standing there,
she did not cry. It was quieter than crying. It was recognition. The feeling of hearing your
own voice after years of speaking only for other people.

**[PAUSE]**

**[ENERGY UP, gentle, the turn]**

So Claire did something small. She kept her hands in the water, and she asked her body one
question. Out loud, in the empty kitchen, feeling slightly foolish. She said:

**[EMPHASIS, soft]** "How are you, darling?"

**[SCENE: the faintest warmth returns to the light. Not a flood. A single degree.]**

**[PAUSE]**

And her body answered. Not in words. In a loosening. Her shoulders came down from around her
ears where they had lived for a decade. Her breath went one inch deeper. And somewhere low
and quiet, in a place she had stopped visiting, something flickered. Not desire for him. Not
yet. **[EMPHASIS]** Desire for her own life. The pilot light, still lit, after eleven years.

**[PAUSE]**

That is the moment. Right there. Hands in the dishwater on an ordinary Tuesday. Not the
bedroom. Not the hotel. The sink. Because desire does not come back through pressure, my
love. She comes back through being asked. Through safety. Through one honest question in an
empty kitchen.

---

## ACT FOUR: WHAT CHANGED (6:15 to 7:30)

**[SCENE: small daily moments, warming slowly. Claire choosing a different coffee. Sitting
in the sun for four minutes. Saying a quiet "no" to something. Buying flowers for no one but
herself.]**

**[ENERGY UP]**

Here is what Claire did not do next. She did not rush upstairs and initiate. She did not fix
her marriage that afternoon. That is the mistake, and she did not make it.

**[PAUSE]**

Instead, she asked the question again the next day. And the next. How are you, darling. What
would feel good right now. Not what would be productive. What would feel good. The first
answer was always "I don't know." She learned to wait for the second answer. The second
answer was the true one.

**[SCENE: weeks passing, the palette warming a little more each time. Then, one evening, her
hand reaching across the duvet first.]**

**[ENERGY DOWN, tender]**

And somewhere around week seven, on an evening no one had planned, Claire reached for him
first. For the first time in three years. **[PAUSE]** Not because she should. Not to keep
the peace. Because a woman who has started wanting her own life again has wanting to spare.

**[EMPHASIS]** The order matters. She did not find desire for him and then get her life back.
She got her life back, and desire followed her home.

## Production and retention notes

- **Runtime:** aim for 7 to 9 minutes. Long enough to bank watch time, short enough to hold
  retention. If a scene sags in the read-aloud test, cut it before you animate it.
- **Pattern interrupts** land at roughly 0:20 (pull back), 0:45 (promise), 0:45 to 2:30
  montage colour drain, 2:30 (bedroom), 3:00 (hard cut to advice), 4:15 (empty Tuesday),
  5:30 (the question), 6:15 (daily moments), 7:30 (close). That is a visual or tonal change
  every 20 to 40 seconds.
- **The tension beat** is the sink recognition at 4:15 to 5:30, deliberately in the middle
  third, quiet rather than loud. Hold the silence in the voiceover. Do not rush it.
- **Advertiser-friendly:** the story is emotional and relational, never graphic. The bedroom
  scenes imply, they do not show. This keeps the video out of limited-ads territory while
  still landing the desire theme.
- **The keyword CTA (DESIRE)** should trigger the same email-capture automation as the Shorts
  pipeline in the roadmap, so this long-form feeds the list, not just the watch-hour count.
- **Thumbnail and title** for this exact video are worked out in `youtube-monetization-roadmap.md`,
  Section 5. Test two of those thumbnails against two of the titles when it goes live.
