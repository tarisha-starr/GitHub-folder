# Caption & Comment Strategy — Tarisha Starr

**Niche:** Women over 40 | Desire, intimacy, body, midlife
**Designed for:** Watch time, comments, shares, saves

---

## Part 1: Caption Templates

### Template 1: The "I See This Every Day" (Authority + relatability)

```
I see this every day in my work.

[Describe the pattern in 2-3 short sentences. Be specific.]

[Reframe it. One sentence that changes everything.]

[Question or CTA]
```

**Example:**
I see this every day in my work. Women who give until there's nothing left for themselves. And they don't even notice when it happened.

You didn't lose yourself on purpose. You just kept saying yes to everyone else until your own voice went quiet.

When was the last time someone asked what YOU need?

---

### Template 2: The "She Said / I Said" (Story format)

```
[Client quote — make it raw and specific]

[Your response — the reframe, the truth, the permission]

[What happened next or what it means]

[CTA]
```

**Example:**
A woman sat across from me and said "I just want to feel like myself again."

I said: "What if the woman you're looking for isn't behind you? What if she's ahead?"

She cried. Not because it was sad. Because it was the first time someone told her she wasn't going backwards.

Does this resonate? Comment 'me' if you're searching too.

---

### Template 3: The "Hard Truth" (Bold + compassionate)

```
[Bold opening statement]

[Explain it. Keep it to 2-3 sentences.]

[Soften with empathy — one sentence]

[CTA]
```

**Example:**
Your desire didn't disappear. You stopped making room for it.

Between the kids, the house, the mental load, and everyone else's feelings... when was the last time you made space for what YOUR body wants?

This isn't blame. It's an invitation. You can start making room again. Right now.

What's one thing you'd make room for if you could?

---

### Template 4: The "Reframe" (Pain hook + truth)

```
[Name the lie she tells herself]
[Replace it with the truth]

[Optional: one more layer of depth]

[CTA]
```

**Example:**
You don't hate your body. You've just stopped listening to her.

She's been sending signals for years. The tension. The exhaustion. The numbness. Those aren't failures. They're messages.

What is she trying to tell you?

---

### Template 5: The "Permission" (Liberation)

```
[Name what she hasn't allowed herself]

[Give explicit permission. Short, direct sentences.]

[Invitation]
```

**Example:**
You're allowed to want more.

More pleasure. More closeness. More of yourself. You're allowed to say "this isn't enough" without feeling guilty. You're allowed to ask for what you need without apologising.

Wanting more doesn't make you selfish. It makes you alive.

Save this for the next time you feel guilty about wanting.

---

### Template 6: The "List" (Symptoms she'll recognise)

```
[Hook: name the category]

[List 3-5 signs, each on its own line]

[Reframe what the signs mean]

[CTA]
```

**Example:**
Signs your body is asking you to slow down:

You can't fall asleep even though you're exhausted.
Your shoulders live next to your ears.
You can't remember the last time you felt relaxed. Actually relaxed.
You say "I'm fine" more than any other sentence.
Intimacy feels like one more thing on the list.

These aren't flaws. They're your body's way of saying: I need you to come back.

How many did you check off? Drop the number.

---

### Template 7: The "Practice" (Save-worthy)

```
[Invitation — gentle, warm]

[The practice — step by step, simple]

[What might come up]

[CTA: save or try tonight]
```

**Example:**
Tonight, before bed, try this.

Put your hand on your belly. Close your eyes. And ask: "How are you doing, darling?"

Don't answer with your head. Let your body answer. It might be a feeling. A colour. A word. Let it come.

This is how you start coming home to yourself.

Save this. Try it tonight. Tell me what came up.

## Part 2: Pinned Comments (Post Under Your Own Content)

Pin one of these under each post to direct the conversation.

### For Mirror/Reframe posts:
- "If this found you today, it's because you needed to hear it. Tell me which line hit hardest."
- "Real talk: how long have you been carrying this? Years? Decades? You can put it down now."
- "I wrote this for the woman who's been saying 'I'm fine' all week. She knows who she is."

### For Story posts:
- "This happens in my practice more than you'd think. You're not the only one."
- "She gave me permission to share this because she wanted you to know you're not alone."
- "I hear some version of this every single week. It's more common than you think."

### For Practice posts:
- "Did you try it? Tell me what came up. I read every comment."
- "No right or wrong answer here. Just honesty."
- "This tiny practice has shifted more than you'd believe. Try it and come back."

### For Spark/trending posts:
- "Controversial? Maybe. True? Tell me I'm wrong."
- "Okay but WHY does this hit so hard?"
- "I'll say it louder for the women in the back."

## Part 3: Engagement Questions (End of Every Post)

### Low-barrier questions (easy to answer, high comment count):
- "Can you relate?"
- "Type 'me' if this is you."
- "Save or share?"
- "Body or head... where do you live most?"
- "Which one hit hardest?"
- "Drop the number."

### Mid-depth questions (require a real answer):
- "When was the last time someone asked what YOU need?"
- "What would you ask for if you weren't afraid?"
- "When did you stop choosing yourself?"
- "What's the one sentence you've been holding back?"
- "What does your body need today?"

### Deep questions (confession-level, long comments):
- "What did you give up that you wish you hadn't?"
- "When did you start performing instead of being yourself?"
- "What would intimacy look like if you were completely honest?"
- "If your body could speak right now, what would she say?"
- "What are you protecting by staying silent?"

## Part 4: Conversation Starters for Stories

Use these in Story polls, question stickers, and sliders:

### Poll stickers:
- "Do you live more in your body or your head?" [Body / Head]
- "When someone asks 'how are you?' do you:" [Tell the truth / Say 'I'm fine']
- "Intimacy is:" [About sex / About being seen]
- "The last time I chose myself:" [This week / I can't remember]

### Question stickers:
- "Finish this sentence: 'I wish someone would...'"
- "What does your body need today? One word."
- "What's the one conversation you keep avoiding?"
- "If you could say one thing to your 25-year-old self, what would it be?"

### Slider stickers:
- "How 'fine' are you really?" [not fine → actually fine]
- "How often do you override what your body is telling you?" [never → daily]
- "How seen do you feel in your relationship?" [invisible → completely seen]

## Part 5: Comment Response Templates

When followers comment, respond in a way that deepens connection and encourages others to comment.

### For "this is me" comments:
- "I see you. And you're not alone in this. How long have you been carrying it?"
- "The fact that you said that out loud? That's the first step. I mean it."

### For vulnerability/sharing:
- "Thank you for sharing this. I hear you. And this matters."
- "This is so honest. I wish more people talked about this."

### For questions:
- "Such a good question. I'm going to make a post about this. Stay tuned."
- "DM me. I want to talk about this properly, not in a comment."

### For disagreement:
- "I appreciate you pushing back on this. What's your experience been?"
- "Fair point. There's nuance here for sure. Tell me more about what you've seen."
