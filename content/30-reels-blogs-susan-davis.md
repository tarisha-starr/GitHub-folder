### What "Bioidentical" Actually Means (And What It Doesn't)

The word "bioidentical" has become one of the most powerful marketing terms in women's health. And one of the most misunderstood.

Here's what it actually means: a bioidentical hormone is structurally identical to the hormones your body naturally produces. The oestradiol in many regulated HRT products IS bioidentical.

Here's what it DOESN'T mean: safer, more natural, or better than other options.

The confusion comes from compounding pharmacies and wellness brands that use "bioidentical" to market unregulated hormone formulations as superior to regulated medications. They imply that regulated hormones are "synthetic" and "unnatural" while their products are "personalised to your body."

### The Timing Window for HRT: What the Evidence Actually Says

If you're going to make an informed decision about menopause hormone therapy, you need to know about the timing window.

Research shows that MHT is safest and most beneficial when started within 10 years of menopause onset, and ideally before age 60. This is sometimes called the "window of opportunity."

Starting within this window is associated with:
- Effective relief of vasomotor symptoms
- Potential cardiovascular benefits (though not enough evidence to use it for prevention)
- Lower risk profile overall

Starting AFTER this window (more than 10 years post-menopause or after age 60) may carry:
- Increased cardiovascular risk
- Higher stroke risk
- Less favourable benefit-to-risk ratio

This doesn't mean you should panic and rush to get a prescription. It means you should have the conversation with your doctor while the window is open.

Professor Susan Davis and other experts recommend that transdermal oestradiol (patches, gels) at low to moderate doses is generally preferred when cardiovascular or blood clot risk is a concern.
