# Your Body Isn't Interested in Intimacy When It's Busy Surviving

I read something today that stopped me in the middle of my morning.

Some nervous systems learned early that connection was reliable. Others learned that connection came with conditions. And many learned that connection was unpredictable, or simply unavailable.

And if your body had to move through life without consistent attunement... without someone noticing your distress, without someone steady enough to offer calm... it learned to regulate alone.

It learned to brace. To over-function. To withdraw. To go quiet. To disappear.

Read that again, darling. Not out of failure. Out of brilliance.

## So much of what you call "stuck" was never stuck at all

I hear it every week. "I don't know what's wrong with me. I've done the work. I've read the books. Why am I still here?"

You're not stuck. You're alone in it.

So much of what we name as stuck is really the absence of co-regulation. It's the absence of someone steady enough for your system to soften beside. Your body was never designed to calm itself in isolation. She was designed to settle next to safety. And if safety wasn't there, she did the next best thing. She held it all herself.

That's not a flaw you need to fix. That's a survival strategy that kept you alive. Honour her for it first.

## Here's what I'm realising in my own body

I'll be honest with you, because I won't ask you to go anywhere I haven't gone.

I'm seeing now that having an unregulated nervous system, on top of being deeply sensitive, has been sitting at the core of everything. Not one issue over here and another over there. The root. The thing underneath the thing.

And it felt like too far a stretch to imagine myself as a radiant, sexual woman with an erotic voice... when I don't yet have a steady nervous system at my centre.

Because here's the truth nobody sells you in a course.

Your body isn't interested in intimacy when she's in survival mode.

She won't open for pleasure while she's scanning for threat. She won't soften into desire while she's braced for the next disappointment. You can light the candles. You can buy the lingerie. You can say all the right things. And she'll still say, quietly, not yet.

She's not broken. She's not frigid. She's not too much or not enough. She's protecting you. And she's waiting for the one thing she's never reliably had. Safety.

## This is the actual order of the work

We get it backwards. We reach for connection first, then wonder why it keeps collapsing.

But it goes like this.

Self-regulation first. Learning to be with your own body. Learning to notice when she braces and to stay with her anyway. Learning what settles her, slowly, on the days nothing feels safe.

Then co-regulation. Borrowing calm from someone steady. Letting your system learn, maybe for the first time, that it doesn't have to do this alone.

And only then, connection. The intimacy. The eros. The radiant, embodied woman you keep reaching for.

Not because you're behind. Because you're building the foundation the other things stand on.

I have more work to do on my own before I bring this to my partner. And I'm not ashamed of that anymore. That's not me failing at intimacy. That's me finally starting where it actually starts.

## The permission I'll offer you

You don't have to perform desire from a body that's still surviving.

You get to come home to her first. To ask her, gently, "how are you doing, darling?" And to stay long enough to hear the answer.

Say it with me. "I'll regulate before I reach."

If your body's been in survival mode for so long you've forgotten what calm even feels like, comment 'me' and I'll send you where to begin.
