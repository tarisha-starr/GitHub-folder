# Step 2 — Brutally Honest Validation

**Idea under review:** *The 7-Day Reconnection Reset* — a $19–$39 done-for-you
daily reset (prompts + exact scripts + short audio) that helps a woman go from
"we're roommates" to feeling close again in one week, and opens into the
$97/month *Roommates to Lovers* membership.

**Target buyer:** Women 40+ in long-term relationships/marriages who feel like
roommates, still love their partner, and want desire and closeness back — without
a big confrontation or dragging him to therapy.

> **VERIFIED** = supported by the web research below. **ASSUMPTION** = reasonable
> but you should confirm with your own audience before betting big.

---

## 1. Buyer pain
- **How painful:** High and identity-level. This isn't "annoying," it's "am I going to feel unwanted for the rest of my life?" That's a top-tier, will-pay-to-fix pain. **VERIFIED** the pattern is widespread and emotionally heavy — therapists describe couples sliding into "logistics mode" and "roommate syndrome" as extremely common in long relationships.
- **How often:** Daily, chronic, and worsening with neglect. It's a slow ache, not a one-time event — which is exactly what justifies a *membership* (ongoing) over a one-off. **VERIFIED** as a gradual, persistent drift.
- **What they do instead now:** Free blog posts and YouTube, hint/nag at their partner, suggest date nights that fizzle, quietly resign, or consider (but resist) couples therapy. Most do *nothing effective*. **VERIFIED** that free content + therapy are the main alternatives; **ASSUMPTION** on the exact proportions for your audience.

## 2. Demand
- **What they search:** Exact-match phrases like *"feel like roommates,"* *"roommate marriage,"* *"reignite the spark,"* *"rebuild intimacy."* The Gottman Institute publishes *"When You Feel Like Roommates But Want to Be Lovers Again"* — your brand name **is** the search term. **VERIFIED.**
- **Similar products that exist:** Spark My Relationship (therapist video course), Esther Perel's *Desire* bundle, Dr. Cheryl Fraser's free masterclass funnel, Naked Marriage SPARK coaching, "Thrive in Love." **VERIFIED.**
- **What buyers already pay:** "Thrive in Love" sells a 90-day course at **exactly $97**; the broader market spans free masterclasses up to multi-hundred-dollar courses and coaching. Your $97/mo membership sits squarely inside proven price territory. **VERIFIED.**

## 3. Competition
- **What competitors do well:** Authority (real therapists/PhDs), depth, polished video, trust. Esther Perel especially owns the "desire" conversation.
- **What they do badly:** They're **heavy**. Long courses, clinical tone, big time commitment, and they often *require both partners to participate*. They overwhelm the exhausted 40+ woman who has 15 minutes and a reluctant husband.
- **Open angle (your wedge):** **Fast, light, done-for-you, and startable solo.** One tiny move a day with the *exact words* to say. Warm best-friend tone, not therapist tone. "You can start tonight, by yourself, without announcing a Relationship Project." Almost nobody owns the *low-friction, scripted, solo-startable* lane. **ASSUMPTION** (strong, but verify by reading competitor reviews for "too long / too clinical / he wouldn't do it" complaints — that's your gap, confirmed).

## 4. Product strength
- **Why they buy:** Low price, low risk, instant clarity, a felt result in 7 days, and it doesn't require a hard conversation. It scratches the "I need to do *something*" itch tonight.
- **Why they might ignore it:** (a) They blame their partner and want *him* fixed, not a self-led plan. (b) "7 days" can read as gimmicky/too-good. (c) Shame — buying it admits there's a problem. (d) Skepticism that anything short can help.
- **What makes it more valuable:** The **exact scripts** (removes the scariest part), **short audio** (press-play ease), a **private/discreet** framing, and a clear bridge: "Day 7 is the beginning — keep going inside the membership." Add the Initiation Scripts as a bump.

## 5. Verdict
- **Rating: 8.5 / 10.** Severe ongoing pain + verified search demand + proven price points + a genuinely open angle. Points lost only because the space is crowded with credible authorities, so your *differentiation and voice* must carry it.
- **Worth building? Yes — build it now.** It's low-effort to produce, validates the funnel cheaply, and naturally sells the recurring membership.
- **Strongest version of the offer:**
  > **The 7-Day Reconnection Reset** — *"One small move a night to go from roommates to lovers again — no big talk, no therapy, no dragging him anywhere. Done-for-you prompts and the exact words to say."*
  > **$27** (with a $17 *Initiation Scripts* order bump) → on Day 7, invite into the
  > **$97/month membership** for the next 30 days of reconnection. Offer a simple
  > "do all 7 days, no change you feel, full refund" guarantee to crush risk.

---

### Honest risks to watch
1. **You are not (presumably) a credentialed therapist.** Don't imply clinical treatment. Position as guided practices and tools, lean on lived experience + your audience's results, and never promise to "fix" serious issues (abuse, addiction, betrayal need pros — say so).
2. **Crowded market.** Voice and specificity are your moat. Be the warm, blunt friend, not the 18th course.
3. **"He won't participate" objection is real.** Bake the solo-startable angle into the product and the sales page — it's your biggest differentiator *and* your biggest objection.
