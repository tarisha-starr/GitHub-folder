# Step 2. Validation (brutally honest)

**Idea:** a free 5-day embodiment challenge that funnels women over 40 into a
$97/month relational membership (the renamed Radiant Woman Circle).

> VERIFIED = supported by research. ASSUMPTION = reasonable, confirm with your
> own numbers.

## 1. Buyer pain
- **How painful:** high and identity-level. Feeling switched off, touched out, or
  like a stranger in your own body is not a minor complaint. It carries grief,
  shame, and fear that "this is just it now". Women pay to change that.
  **VERIFIED** the pattern is widespread; the "roommate" drift and post-40 desire
  drop are heavily documented.
- **How often:** chronic and daily. It does not resolve on its own, which is the
  exact pain profile that justifies an ongoing membership rather than a one-off.
  **VERIFIED** as a gradual, persistent state.
- **What they do instead now:** free YouTube and blogs, hint at their partner,
  blame their hormones, consider therapy and resist it, or quietly give up.
  Most do nothing that works. **VERIFIED** that free content and therapy are the
  main alternatives.

## 2. Demand
- **What they search:** "feel like roommates", "lost my libido after 40",
  "responsive desire", "rebuild intimacy", "low desire menopause". Your existing
  content already ranks against these. **VERIFIED.**
- **Similar products:** monthly intimacy memberships exist (for example Gina
  Marie's "The Spark"), alongside Esther Perel, Spark My Relationship, and many
  1:1 sex and intimacy coaches for women. **VERIFIED.** A women-only monthly
  membership in this space is a proven format, not a gamble.
- **What buyers pay:** $97/month is squarely inside proven territory (your own
  Circle already runs at $97; intimacy courses span free to several hundred).
  **VERIFIED.**

## 3. Competition (and the challenge model itself)
- **What competitors do well:** authority and depth (therapists, PhDs). Esther
  Perel owns "desire" culturally.
- **What they do badly:** most are either heavy clinical courses or generic
  influencer content. Few offer an ongoing, embodied, women-only practice home
  with a live human in the room. Few use a felt-experience challenge as the door.
- **Open angle (yours):** somatic, women-over-40, girlfriend-not-coach voice, a
  felt 5-day taste that proves the work in the body before any pitch. That
  combination is rare. **ASSUMPTION** (strong; confirm by scanning competitor
  reviews for "too clinical / too long / didn't change how I feel").
- **On the funnel mechanics:** challenge funnels are a proven launch model.
  Reported benchmarks: 60 to 75% of challenge graduates accept a community
  invitation (versus 10 to 20% from a cold audience), and challenge-to-paid
  conversion commonly lands in the 5 to 12% range for higher-ticket, higher for
  low-cost continuity. **VERIFIED** as industry norms (your mileage depends on
  list warmth and offer fit). See sources.

## 4. Product strength
- **Why they buy:** a felt result in five days with no risk (free), a women-only
  room, and an ongoing home so the feeling does not fade by Monday.
- **Why they might ignore it:** (a) "another challenge" fatigue, (b) shame about
  the topic, (c) belief that it is too late, (d) wanting their partner fixed
  rather than self-led work, (e) doubt that something embodied can be done over a
  screen.
- **What makes it more valuable:** the live human (you) in the calls, the
  community (shame dies in a group), the monthly cadence, and the clean promise
  that it is relational and ongoing, not a deep solo excavation (which keeps it
  distinct from SERW and lowers the perceived effort).

## 5. Verdict
- **Rating: 9 / 10.** Severe ongoing pain, verified demand, proven price, a
  proven funnel model, an existing warm audience and brand, and a genuinely open
  somatic-relational angle. The only risk is execution and differentiation, both
  of which are handled by the positioning below.
- **Worth building? Yes, and most of it already exists.** You are repackaging a
  proven membership (the Circle) and reusing proven challenge content. This is
  low-build, high-confidence.
- **Strongest version of the offer:**
  > Free 5-day **Come Home Challenge** (felt embodiment taste, opens toward
  > intimacy on Day 5) → on Day 5, invite into **[NEW MEMBERSHIP NAME]**, the
  > ongoing relational practice home for women who want to stay awake in their
  > bodies and warm in their relationships, $97/month, cancel anytime, existing
  > Circle members roll in.

### Honest risks to watch
1. **Cannibalising SERW.** Keep the membership relational and at practice depth.
   Use the one-line test: deep solo healing of the woman herself is SERW; ongoing
   relational practice she brings into her intimacy is the membership.
2. **Challenge fatigue.** Your warm voice and the somatic felt-result are the
   differentiators. Lead with the experience, not the information.
3. **Free vs paid challenge.** Free fills the room and grows the list fastest but
   pulls lower-intent sign-ups. A small fee ($19 to $27) filters for buyers and
   lifts conversion. **Decide based on whether the goal is list growth or
   immediate membership revenue.**

# Step 3. The smallest useful version

**The exact result:** by Day 5 she has felt her body wake up (sensation,
pleasure, her own wanting) and has brought one moment of that aliveness toward
her partner. She ends with proof that the feeling is available to her, and a
clear door to keep it going.

**Format (v1):** a free Facebook group + 5 short daily teachings (pre-recorded,
12 to 18 min) + 3 reusable practice audios + 5 daily emails + a Day 5 membership
invitation. Nothing else.

**Must include:** one short body practice per day, a daily group share prompt,
the live human element on at least the close, the safety note, and the single
Day 5 pitch.

**Remove for v1:** an app, a course platform, a workbook PDF, partner worksheets,
the desire quizzes, the Matrix and Blueprints (paid-product territory), and any
mid-challenge selling.

**Order:** body → senses → body-peace → erotic voice → invite him in. Self first,
relationship last.

**Completion checklist (hers):** did all 5 practices, shared in the group at
least twice, listened to the 3 audios, did the Day 5 invitation, chose whether to
continue in the membership.

**Final deliverables:** 5 teaching videos, 3 practice audios, 5 emails, 1 group,
1 Day 5 invitation, 1 membership checkout link.

**Fastest version today:** the full content is written in `5-day-challenge.md`.
Record the 5 teachings on your phone or pull edited clips from the Aug 2025
recordings, set up the group, schedule the 5 emails, done.

**Better version later:** a branded opt-in page, a printable one-page practice
card, a quiz front-end, and live calls instead of pre-recorded.
