# Comment Section Strategy

The algorithm tests every post in the first 60 minutes. If nobody engages,
the post dies. The comment section is what feeds the algorithm during that
window. This applies at every level (1, 2, 3) and to every post.

## The system

1. Publish the post.
2. Within 5 minutes, drop **5 to 8 value comments under your own post.**
   Each comment is a mini-lesson with:
   - a title (bold one-liner)
   - the problem
   - the solution
3. Reply to **every** comment for the first 2 hours.
4. In your replies, ask a question — keep the thread alive, don't close it.

## Why it works

- Each self-comment looks like free content; people screenshot and share them.
- High comment count + fast comment velocity is the strongest engagement
  signal the algorithm reads.
- Replies-with-questions double the comment count without adding new viewers.

## What the comments should look like

Treat the comment section like a free mini-course. Each comment is a node:

> **The mistake almost everyone makes**
> Most women try to *think* their way back to desire.
> Desire isn't cognitive. It's a body state. Start there.

> **The 30-second test**
> Stand still. Breathe. Notice what your body wants — not what you think it should want.
> That gap is where the work is.

5 to 8 of these per post.

## Per-level emphasis

- **Level 1 (0–1K):** This is how you punch above your weight. A weak post
  with strong comments outperforms a strong post with no comments.
- **Level 2 (1K–10K):** This compounds the doubling-down — winning posts get
  amplified harder when the comment section is loaded.
- **Level 3 (10K+):** This is why posts hit millions. Don't skip it just
  because the post is already pre-loaded.

## Authoring prompt

Use this prompt verbatim when generating self-comments for a post:

> You are a content strategist. Below is a social media post (hook + caption).
> Generate **6 self-comments** I will post under my own post in the first 5
> minutes after publishing. Each comment must be a mini-lesson with a bold
> title (one line), a problem (one sentence), and a solution (one to two
> sentences). Comments must extend the post's idea — not repeat it. Tone
> matches `content/formula.md` and `content/hooks-rules.md`.
