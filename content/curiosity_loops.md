# Prompt 4: Add curiosity loops

Use this after a caption, script, or email is drafted. Paste the block
below into ChatGPT along with the draft. It strengthens retention
without tipping into clickbait or breaking the voice in `style_guide.md`.

```
Add curiosity loops to this content without making it clickbait.

I want the audience to feel like they need to keep reading or watching.

Add:

Open loops

Delayed payoff

Micro questions

Pattern interrupts

Stronger transitions

Keep it natural and not robotic.
```

## What each lever means in her voice

- **Open loops** — name a tension or contradiction early, resolve it
  later. "There's a reason she stopped reaching for him. It's not what
  you think."
- **Delayed payoff** — promise the insight, then earn it across 2-3
  beats before delivering. No bait, no withholding for sport.
- **Micro questions** — short, rhythmic, one-line questions that pull
  the reader forward. "Sound familiar? Still with me?"
- **Pattern interrupts** — a single line that breaks cadence. A blunt
  truth, a one-word sentence, an ellipsis where the reader expected a
  conclusion.
- **Stronger transitions** — never "Furthermore" or "However". Use
  "And here's the part most women miss." or "But." on its own line.

## Guardrails

- Keep contractions, British/NZ spelling, no em-dashes.
- Don't manufacture mystery. The loop has to actually pay off in the
  same piece.
- One open loop per caption is enough. Two is the ceiling for a long
  email or video script.
- If the loop reads like a thumbnail headline ("You won't BELIEVE..."),
  cut it. It's clickbait.
