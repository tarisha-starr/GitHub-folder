# Radiant Women's Circle — Two-Part Programme: Needs, Wants & Desires

**Two live 2-hour sessions on Zoom.** Part of the "Become a Sexually Empowered
Radiant Woman" course.

The arc across the two sessions:

- **Session 1 — Coming Home to Yourself.** First we find it. Why you don't even know
  your own needs (the invisible identity), then the journey from what you DON'T want,
  to what you DO want, to your deepest desire.
- **Session 2 — Speaking It So You're Heard.** Then we voice it. How you're wired, how
  to ask so you inspire instead of demand, and how to let the people who love you
  actually give you what you want.

You find your desire in Session 1. You learn to speak it and be met in Session 2.

## Session 1 — Coming Home to Yourself (120 min)

**Intention:** she leaves knowing what she doesn't want, what she does want, and one
deep desire she's never said out loud, and understanding *why* it's been so hard to
know.

| Time | Block | Run this |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00–0:12 | **Arrive & soften** | `radiant-circle...` Part 1 (candle, agreements, Soften the Jaw, one-word go-round) |
| 0:12–0:37 | **Teaching: the invisible woman** | `teaching...` Part A §1 (Zammit invisible identity + how we train others not to see us) and the "three words" intro (needs / wants / desires) |
| 0:37–0:52 | **Breakout 1: How I make myself invisible** | `teaching...` Exercise 1 (pairs, 10 min + 5 min main-room share) |
| 0:52–1:10 | **What I DON'T want** | `radiant-circle...` Part 3 (frame, journal, dyad share) |
| 1:10–1:18 | **Break** | Cameras off, stretch, water |
| 1:18–1:38 | **What I DO want** | `radiant-circle...` Part 4 (turn the no into a yes, journal, speak it out loud in dyads) |
| 1:38–1:55 | **My deepest desire** | `radiant-circle...` Part 5 (guided drop-in + deep journal) |
| 1:55–2:00 | **Claim it & close** | `radiant-circle...` Part 6 claiming round + Claim Your Power affirmation |

**Take-home:** the Session 1 take-home practice (say one clean want a day; whisper your
deepest desire before sleep). Tell them Session 2 is where they learn to make those
wants heard, so they arrive already practising.

## Session 2 — Speaking It So You're Heard (120 min)

**Intention:** she leaves able to turn a desire into a clean, warm, specific ask that
inspires her partner to give, instead of a complaint that makes him withdraw.

| Time | Block | Run this |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00–0:12 | **Arrive, soften & recap** | Soften the Jaw again; one line each: "One desire I found last time was..." |
| 0:12–0:35 | **Teaching: how you're wired** | `teaching...` Part A §2–3 (diffuse awareness vs single focus; why "sharing" isn't "asking"; nagging vs a real request) |
| 0:35–0:50 | **Breakout 2: Sharing vs Asking** | `teaching...` Exercise 2 (pairs, 10 min + 5 min share) |
| 0:50–1:06 | **Teaching: the Great Ask** | `teaching...` Part A §4–5 (the four-step Great Ask; frustration is a gap, not a fact) |
| 1:06–1:14 | **Break** | Cameras off, stretch, water |
| 1:14–1:30 | **Breakout 4: The Great Ask, out loud** | `teaching...` Exercise 4 (pairs, rehearse the four steps) |
| 1:30–1:44 | **Teaching: inspire, don't demand** | `teaching...` Part A §6–8 (appreciation unlocks generosity; receiving; letting your man succeed at giving you what you want) |
| 1:44–1:58 | **Breakout 6: Demand into invitation** | `teaching...` Exercise 6 (same want as a demand, then as an invitation that hands him the win) |
| 1:58–2:00 | **Claiming close** | Each woman: "This week, the one clean want I'm going to actually say is..." Circle answers: "You're allowed." |

**Optional swap:** if a group is more focused on tone than mechanics, run Exercise 5
(Appreciation first) in the 1:44 slot instead of Exercise 6. Both live in `teaching...`.

**Take-home:** the teaching-module take-home (one clean, specific, appreciation-first
request a day; watch the generosity come back).

## Running two 2-hour sessions on Zoom — practical notes

- **Spacing:** run the two sessions a week apart if you can. The gap lets Session 1's
  take-home practice ripen, so Session 2 has lived material to work with.
- **Breakouts:** pairs. Assign Partner A / B by first-name alphabetical order so no
  breakout time is lost deciding. Broadcast the prompt to all rooms before opening, and
  re-broadcast the swap instruction at the halfway mark.
- **Timers:** set the Zoom breakout countdown so women hear the warning before rooms
  close. Always bring them back to the main room for the go-round; that shared
  witnessing is where the shift locks in.
- **Container:** re-read the circle agreements at the top of both sessions (confidential,
  we witness rather than fix, you can always pass, no such thing as too much here).
- **Hold it slow.** Both sessions move from the head into the body. Long pauses are the
  work, not dead air. If a round is alive, add a few minutes and trim the next teaching
  block.
