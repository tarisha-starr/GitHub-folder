# Radiant Women's Circle — Finding Your Needs & Wants

**A 90-minute session (also runs as the core of Session 1 in the two-part programme)**
**From the "Become a Sexually Empowered Radiant Woman" course (10 weeks / 3 months)**

> This runs standalone at 90 minutes, or as the heart of the 2-hour **Session 1** in
> `00-two-part-programme-overview.md` (paired with the Zammit invisible-identity
> teaching and breakout). See that file for the full two-session running order.

> The arc of tonight: we start with what you *don't* want. Because the "no" is
> the doorway. Then we move into what you *do* want. And then we go all the way
> down, into what you *desire*. Your deepest, most honest desires.

Most women in this circle have spent decades being told not to be too much. To
hint, to hope, to wait for someone to read their mind. To be polite. To make do.

Tonight we practise something different. Tonight your desire is allowed.

---

## What you need

- A circle of chairs or cushions, candle in the centre
- Each woman brings a journal and pen (or provide them)
- Soft music for arrival and for the journaling rounds
- Tissues in reach
- A talking piece for the closing round (a stone, a flower, anything held)
- Optional: warm tea for the arrival

**Circle agreements to read out at the start:**
- What's shared here stays here.
- We don't fix, advise, or rescue each other. We witness.
- You can always pass.
- No such thing as too much in this room.

## Part 1 — Arrive & soften (0:00–0:15)

Welcome them. Light the candle. Read the circle agreements.

Then, before any talking, bring them into the body. Read this slowly, with long
pauses. This is the **Soften the Jaw** practice from the course.

> Let your eyes close, darling.
>
> Feel your feet on the floor. Feel where your body meets the chair.
>
> Now soften your jaw. Let it drop, just a little. Drop your shoulders down away
> from your ears. Unclench your belly. Let your pelvic floor release.
>
> Feel that?
>
> That's your feminine essence coming back online. She doesn't live in the
> tension. She lives in the softening.
>
> Take three slow breaths down into your belly. With every out-breath, let a
> little more of the day fall away. The to-do list. The school run. The person
> you had to be all day.
>
> You don't have to be her in here.

Let them sit in the quiet for a moment. Then invite eyes to open, slowly.

**Go-round (one word each):** "How are you arriving tonight? One word." No fixing,
no story. Just witnessing where everyone is.

---

## Part 2 — Why we lost the thread (0:15–0:25)

Teach this in your own voice. The bones of it:

> Here's what I see every week in my work. A woman sits across from me and I ask
> her a simple question. "What do you want?" And she goes blank.
>
> Not because she's empty. Because she's out of practice.
>
> Somewhere along the way most of us learned it wasn't safe to want. We learned
> to be polite. To not be too much. To put everyone else's needs first and call
> it love. We got so good at reading everyone else's needs that we lost the
> signal for our own.
>
> And here's the thing about desire. It's not a burden. It's not selfish. It's a
> gift. Your desire is the compass. It's how life moves you toward what's yours.
>
> But you can't get to the big, tender, honest wants straight away. If I ask you
> for your deepest desire right now, most of you will freeze. So we don't start
> there.
>
> We start with what you DON'T want. Because the "no" is easy. The "no" you can
> feel in your body immediately. And every "no" is pointing at a "yes" hiding
> just behind it.

Keep it short. The teaching sets up the work; the work is where the shift happens.

---

## Part 3 — What I DON'T want (0:25–0:45)

**Frame it (1 min):**

> We begin with the "no" because your body already knows it. You don't have to
> figure it out. The tight jaw, the heavy chest, the sigh you make on the drive
> home. Your body has been keeping a list.

**Journal round — 7 minutes, music on.** Read the prompts, then let them write.
Tell them: don't think it, feel it. Let the pen move faster than the mind.

- What I'm tired of pretending is...
- What I'm done tolerating is...
- The story I'm done telling myself is...
- In my relationship, in my body, in my life... what I no longer want is...

Encourage them to be specific and a little wild. "Let it be uncensored. Nobody
reads this but you."

**Body check (2 min):**

> Put a hand where the "no" lives. Chest? Throat? Belly? Just breathe into it.
> That "no" has been protecting you. It's not a problem. It's information.

**Share — dyads, 10 minutes (5 each).** Turn to the woman beside you. One speaks,
one witnesses. No advice, no "me too" story. When you're the listener, your only
job is to let her be heard. Swap at the halfway bell.

Close the part:

> Notice how much clearer you feel already. That's what a clean "no" does. It
> makes space. And into that space, we're about to invite the "yes".

---

## Part 4 — What I DO want (0:45–1:05)

**Frame it (2 min):**

> Now we flip it. Every "no" you just named is holding a "yes" behind it. If you
> don't want to feel invisible, then you want to feel seen. If you don't want to
> keep going through the motions, then you want aliveness, touch, pleasure.
>
> This is where we stop hinting and start naming. Because your partner is not a
> mind reader. And your life can't deliver a want you've never let yourself say.

Give the reframe out loud, the way you teach it in **Speak Your Desire**: not a
complaint, not a hint. A clean, warm, specific want.

- Not "you never touch me" → "I want to be kissed slowly tonight."
- Not "I'm always exhausted" → "I want one morning that belongs only to me."

**Journal round — 8 minutes, music on.** Read the prompts slowly:

- What I really want more of is...
- The kind of touch I'm craving is...
- A way I want to receive more is...
- The pleasure I'm allowing more of is...
- One thing I want to do just for me is...
- If I trusted myself completely, today I would...

Invite them to finish that last one three times. "Watch what wants to come out."

**Speak it out loud — this is the medicine (8 min).** Back into dyads. This time
each woman speaks her wants *out loud* to her partner, starting each one with
**"I want..."** Full sentences. No apologising, no shrinking, no "this is silly
but..."

Coach them:

> Say it like it's allowed. Because it is. Notice the urge to soften it, laugh it
> off, take it back. Don't. Let her hear you want it. Let YOU hear you want it.
> Your nervous system is listening.

The listener's only response when she finishes: **"I hear you. You're allowed to
want that."**

Bring the circle back together. Quick go-round: "What happened in your body when
you said it out loud?"

---

## Part 5 — My deepest desire (1:05–1:22)

Now we go underneath the wants, to the desire the wants are really pointing at.

**Guided drop-in (5 min).** Eyes closed, hand on the belly, hand on the heart.
Read slowly, lots of space:

> Come back to the body. Soften the jaw again. Breathe down low.
>
> Bring to mind one of the wants you spoke out loud tonight. Hold it gently.
>
> Now ask it, like you'd ask a child... "And if I had that, what would it give
> me? What would I really feel?"
>
> Maybe the want was for touch. And underneath it, the desire is to feel wanted.
> To feel chosen. Maybe the want was for a morning alone. And underneath it, the
> desire is to matter. To belong to yourself.
>
> Keep asking. "And underneath that... what's the deepest thing?" Let it come.
> Don't reach for the impressive answer. Reach for the true one.
>
> This is your desire. Not the surface one. The one your whole life has been
> quietly organised around.

Let them sit in it. Then:

> Put your hand a little firmer on your heart. And silently, just for you, name
> it. "My deepest desire is..."

**Journal round — 7 minutes.** Music low. The deep prompts:

- Underneath everything, what I most deeply desire is...
- What my partner doesn't know I wish for is...
- The desire that scared me most as a young woman was... (write to her like you'd
  write to a daughter — what does she need to hear from you now?)
- If I trusted life completely, I would...

This one can bring tears. That's not a problem to fix. Let the room hold it. Have
tissues close.

---

## Part 6 — Claim it & close (1:22–1:30)

Bring everyone back to the circle. Talking piece in the centre.

**The claiming round.** One at a time, each woman takes the talking piece and
speaks **one** desire out loud to the whole circle. It can be a want or the deep
desire, whatever she's ready to claim. She begins with:

> "I'm a radiant woman, and I desire..."

The circle's only response, together, warm: **"You're allowed."**

No commentary, no discussion. Just claimed, witnessed, and passed on. This is the
most powerful eight minutes of the night. A room full of women hearing their own
desire spoken out loud and met with "you're allowed" instead of "who do you think
you are."

**Close with the Claim Your Power affirmation** from Week 1 of the course. Have
them stand if they can, hand on heart, say it together:

> "I'm powerful. I can create life. I can create the relationship I desire. I can
> create the life I desire. The power is in my hands."

Blow out the candle together. Done.

---

## Take-home for the week

Send them off with one practice, so the circle keeps working after they leave:

> Every day this week, tell one person one clean, warm, specific thing you want.
> Not a hint. Not a complaint. A desire. "I want to be held when I get home." "I
> want the last piece." "I want to be kissed slowly."
>
> And each night, before sleep, whisper to yourself the deepest desire you named
> tonight. Your nervous system is listening. You're teaching it that your desire
> is safe.

## Facilitator notes

- **Hold the pace slow.** The whole session is designed to move from the head into
  the body. If you rush, they stay in the head. Long pauses are the work.
- **The "no" first is deliberate.** Don't be tempted to skip to the wants. Women
  who freeze at "what do you want?" open right up at "what are you done
  tolerating?" The no clears the runway.
- **Protect the shares.** The moment someone starts fixing or advising, gently
  bring it back: "Let's just witness her." That safety is the whole reason the
  deep desires can come out.
- **Tears are welcome, not an emergency.** Especially in Part 5. Let the woman
  beside her put a hand on her back. Don't rush to soothe. Being witnessed in it
  is the healing.
- **Timing is a guide, not a cage.** If a round is alive, give it another few
  minutes and trim the take-home chat. Read the room.
