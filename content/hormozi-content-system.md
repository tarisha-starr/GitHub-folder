# Hormozi Content Brain: Tarisha Edition

A content system for attention, trust, and inbound demand. Built from
the Hormozi framework, filtered through ME.md so every line sounds like
Tarisha, not like a coach on LinkedIn.

The job of this file: give her a content brain she can run on repeat.
Painful truths her women already feel but won't say out loud. Mistakes
they're paying for in time, money, and aliveness. Beliefs the culture
sold them that have cost them their bodies, their marriages, and their
mornings.

Each idea is paired with three hooks, an example pattern from her
practice, and a subtle line back to her offer (SERW, Love Adventure
retreats with Mark, Radiant Woman Circle) or her authority.

---

## How to use this

1. Pick one idea a day. Don't blend two.
2. Lead with the hook. The hook is the whole post if you're tired.
3. Drop in your own client moment from the day. The example here is a
   scaffold, your moment is the body.
4. End with a comment prompt CTA. Never "link in bio".
5. Run the draft through the voice formula in ME.md section 1:
   question to "we" → personal moment → voice the resistance → puncture
   with a rhetorical question → punchy invitation.
6. Vary sentence length. Short, long, short, medium, short.

### 1. "The marriage is fine" is the most dangerous sentence in a long-term relationship

**Contradiction.** Fine is not safe. Fine is the noise a relationship
makes on its way to roommates.

**Explanation.** Women over 40 use "fine" as a sedative. He's fine. We're
fine. The sex is fine. Fine is the word we reach for when we don't want
to feel the thing underneath. And the thing underneath does not go away
because we named it fine. It goes underground. It comes back as low
desire, frozen shoulders, or a quiet plan to leave at 55.

**What this looks like.** A woman books a session and tells me everything
is fine for ten minutes. Then I ask, when did you last cry in his arms?
And she cries for the next forty.

**Hooks.**
1. "Fine" is the word a marriage uses on its way to roommates.
2. If you've said "we're fine" three times this week, this is for you.
3. You don't have a communication problem. You have a "fine" problem.

**Offer connection.** Love Adventure exists for couples who are done
calling it fine. SERW exists for the women who can't get him there yet.

# Part C. Ten beliefs that are wrong but widely accepted

These are cultural defaults. Repeat them in your audience's voice, then

**Contradiction.** Desire doesn't fade. It changes shape. Nobody taught

**Explanation.** Twenties desire is loud and outward. Forties desire is

**What this looks like.** A woman in her fifties tells me her desire

**Hooks.**

**Contradiction.** The spark didn't die. It got buried under years of
