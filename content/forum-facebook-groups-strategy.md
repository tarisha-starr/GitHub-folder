## Hook

Meta Just Quietly Launched a Reddit Clone for Facebook Groups. Here's What It Means for Anyone Selling on Social

## Post

While most of the coaching and creator world was busy posting Reels, Meta dropped a brand new app called Forum.

No press release. No fanfare. It just showed up in the App Store on May 21, 2026.

This isn't a feature update. It's a standalone app, a dedicated home for Facebook Groups, completely separate from the main Facebook experience. And if you run a group for clients, it changes the math on how you grow it.

Here's everything you need to know, plus exactly how to play it.

### What Forum actually is

Meta's own App Store listing describes Forum as "a dedicated space for the conversations that matter most to you," built for the groups users already belong to and the ones they've yet to discover.

Translation: it's a Reddit clone bolted onto Facebook Groups.

Most outlets covering the launch (MacRumors, Engadget, BetaNews) have drawn the same comparison, niche community discussions, real-people recommendations, question-and-answer style content. It's giving early-2000s forum vibes with a modern AI layer on top.

This isn't Meta's first try. They launched a standalone Facebook Groups app years ago and killed it in 2017. The difference this time? AI.

### How it works

**The feed.** Forum's main feed surfaces conversations from your existing Groups instead of mixing them with friends' posts, Pages, and whatever the algorithm thinks you might tolerate. No more digging through cousin Karen's vacation photos to find your group's posts.

**Onboarding.** When you log in for the first time, the app asks what you want to see more of, meaning it'll also surface posts from Groups you've never joined based on the interests you pick. This is the Reddit-style discovery layer.

**Sync.** Anything you post in Forum syncs to the main Facebook app, and vice versa. You need an existing Facebook account to sign in. No double work.

**Anonymity.** Forum supports anonymised usernames for public interactions, but group admins can still see the real identities behind those accounts. Members can hide. Admins still know exactly who they're talking to.

### The two AI features (this is the real story)

This is where Forum actually matters.

**1. Ask** ... an AI tool that pulls answers from across all the Groups on the platform so users don't have to search each community individually. Think: someone types "What's the best CRM for tracking DMs?" and the AI scrapes posts across every relevant group and returns a synthesised answer with sources. (PS. the Answer is GroupTrack)

**2. Admin AI Assistant** ... an AI built specifically for moderators. Per Meta's own listing, it helps admins manage groups, moderate content, and "keep their communities healthy, all while maintaining control."

Both features are exclusive to Forum. Neither exists in the main Facebook app.

### Availability, the messy details

- **iOS:** Live now. Requires iOS 18.0 or later.
- **Android:** Conflicting reports. Some outlets confirm Play Store availability, others say iOS only. Best read: iOS confirmed, Android unconfirmed.
- **Regions:** Limited rollout, mostly U.S. for now.
- **Status:** Meta confirmed Forum is still in testing. Their spokesperson said they "test lots of new products publicly to see what people find interesting and useful."

So it could expand fast... or quietly die like the 2017 version.

### "Wait, why not just use the Groups feed filter in the regular app?"

Fair question. The main Facebook app already has a Groups tab. Here's what Forum actually does differently, and why it matters for selling:

The Groups feed filter shows you posts from groups you're already in. That's it. It's a filtered view of the same content.

Forum does four things the Groups filter can't:

1. **Ask AI** ... synthesises answers across all groups, including ones you're not in. There's no equivalent on the main app.
2. **Interest-based discovery** ... surfaces posts from groups you've never joined based on the topics you picked during onboarding. The Groups filter only shows you your own groups. Forum shows your stuff to *cold* people.
3. **Admin AI assistant** ... only lives in Forum. Not in the main app.
4. **Zero distraction surface** ... no marketplace, no reels, no friend pings. People who open Forum are there to engage with communities. Higher intent traffic per session.

The Groups feed is a viewing mode. Forum is a different distribution channel with its own discovery engine. That's the real story.

### What this means if you run a group for clients

The herd is about to miss this play. Here's what's actually changing for coaches, creators, network marketers, and service providers running Facebook Groups:

**1. Your group is about to get a second front door.** Members no longer have to wade through the FB feed to find you. In Forum, your group *is* the feed. Higher visibility for active members, no algorithm tax.

**2. "Ask" turns your group's content into search results.** When the AI surfaces answers from across groups, the group with the most useful, well-titled, search-friendly posts becomes the de facto authority. Whoever optimises for this first wins outsized visibility.

**3. Non-members will see your posts.** Interest-based discovery means cold prospects who said "small business marketing" during onboarding might see your group's content without ever joining. New top-of-funnel.

**4. Admins keep identity visibility.** Anonymous-looking members aren't actually anonymous to you. You can still see real names, still track them in your pipeline, still personalise outreach. Nothing breaks.

**5. The Admin AI could eat a chunk of community manager workflows.** Welcome posts, rule enforcement, surfacing unanswered questions... Meta's going to automate this. Worth testing before paying a VA to do it.

**6. The catch.** If Forum cannibalises the main FB feed for group users, casual lurkers who scroll Facebook (your warm audience that hasn't joined yet) might see your group content *less* in the main feed as Meta pushes engagement to Forum. Net: more depth with members, possibly less reach to non-members in the main app.

### The con nobody else is going to tell you

GroupTrack doesn't work in Forum yet. If you're running your pipeline through GroupTrack (and you should be), keep doing the bulk of your tracking, tagging, and DM workflow inside the main Facebook app for now. Forum is for testing, AI experimentation, and visibility plays, not for replacing your CRM workflow. We'll have an update when the integration is live.

### How early movers win the biggest

The window for first-mover advantage on Forum is measured in weeks, not months. Here's where the leverage actually is:

**1. Rewrite your group post titles as questions.** Ask AI is going to favour content that matches how people actually search. "5 tips for closing DMs" becomes "What's the best way to close a sales conversation in DMs?" Same content. Massively different surfacing odds.

**2. Become the answer in your niche.** Every time someone asks Ask a question in your space and your group's post is the source, you just got introduced to a stranger as the authority. Post answer-shaped content to common questions in your industry. Be the source the AI cites.

**3. Test the cold reach.** Post the same content in your group on Forum vs the main app this week. Track which one gets non-member engagement (comments from people who weren't already in your group). That's the Forum tax... or the Forum gift, depending on the result.

**4. Use the admin AI to audit your group right now.** Even if you don't keep using it, getting its take on your community health, unanswered questions, and engagement patterns is free competitive intelligence.

**5. Don't migrate. Add.** Forum isn't replacing the main Facebook app. It's a second front door. Don't pull your effort out of where your buyers actually live. Extend into Forum as a layer, not a swap.

The people who win this aren't the ones who go all-in on Forum. They're the ones who post about it, test it publicly, and own the conversation while everyone else waits for the official Meta announcement.

That announcement might never come. By the time it does, the first-mover window is already closed.
