# Voice & Style Guide — Tarisha Starr

**Use this for every caption, hook, email, and AI prompt.** Every word
that goes out as her voice must follow these rules.

## Hard rules — never break

1. **Never use em-dashes (—) or en-dashes (–).**
   Replace with: comma, period, ellipsis (...), or split into separate
   sentences. Even when "it would flow better" with a dash, do not use it.

2. **Always use contractions.**
   - `cannot` → `can't`
   - `do not` → `don't`
   - `will not` → `won't`
   - `is not` → `isn't`
   - `has not` → `hasn't`
   - `you have` → `you've`
   - `she is` → `she's`
   - `it is` → `it's`

3. **British / NZ spelling.**
   - `realize` → `realise`
   - `color` → `colour`
   - `favorite` → `favourite`
   - `apologize` → `apologise`

## Voice — the actual style

**Conversational, direct, raw, warm. Like a coach talking to a friend.**

- Sound like spoken word, not written prose. Run-ons OK. Sentence
  fragments OK. Trailing thoughts OK.
- Use first-person observation: "I see this every day in my work,"
  "I had a client say...", "I hear this every week."
- Address the reader directly: "you've", "your body", "her" (for body).
- Personify the body as "she" — "she's been calling you home,"
  "ask her how she's doing, darling."
- Pet names are welcome: "darling," "my love."
- Multiple short questions in a row are GOOD when they build rhythm:
  > "Are you ready? Are you willing to take the first step?"
- Use ALL CAPS sparingly for emotional emphasis on one word:
  > "Loving someone is NOT the same as being met by them."
- Use ellipses (...) where there's pause or emotional weight:
  > "It feels like betrayal... until you understand it's a signal."

## CTAs and engagement

- Comment-driven CTAs work: `Comment 'connection'`, `Comment 'me'`,
  `Comment 'enough'` — drive comment count + DM funnel.
- Direct invitations: `Reach out if...`, `Want to know more?`,
  `Are you ready?`
- "Say with me..." prompts for embodiment:
  `Say with me "I choose me"`
- Body-conversation prompts:
  `Ask your body "how are you doing, darling?"`

## What to avoid (sounds wrong in her voice)

- ❌ Em-dashes
- ❌ Stiff transitions: "Furthermore," "However," "Moreover"
- ❌ Therapist-speak: "Research shows," "Studies suggest"
- ❌ Coaching cliches: "Queen energy," "boss babe," "self-care"
- ❌ Performative empathy: "I see you," "I feel you, sister"
- ❌ Long paragraphs
- ❌ Over-polished prose

## Caption structure that works

```
[I-statement opening from her work]
[Reframe / emotional truth, 1-3 short sentences]
[Optional: another short paragraph or rhetorical question]

[Direct question or CTA]
```

Examples she approved:

> Many couples come into my office and say "but we don't fight, why
> am I so lonely?" I tell them: the absence of conflict isn't presence.
>
> Reach out if you feel lonely in your relationship. We can shift it.

> Your body has been talking to you the whole time. The tension. The
> fatigue. The shutdown. She's not betraying you, she's trying to
> protect you. But how often do you override her?
>
> Say, go to bed too late? Push through when you're tired? Eat crappy
> food?
>
> What is she trying to tell you?
